A week-by-week planning template for building retention into your NHA CCMA course — so exam prep isn't the first time students see content twice.
NHA CCMA Aligned 18-Week Framework Any Classroom Structure Print or Fill Digitally Free Resource
How to use this template: This is your retention planning layer — it goes on top of whatever pacing guide you already use. For each week, fill in your current module, your daily recall topic, your callback Friday topic (a module from 2–3 weeks prior), and any check-off or milestone that week. You don't have to plan every question in advance. Just knowing which module you're reaching back to each week is enough. Weeks 1–5 are pre-filled as an example — customize them to match your own sequence.
Three Things to Fill In Each Week
1
Daily Recall Topic
The concept you'll ask about at the start of class — no notes, out loud. Should be from the current module OR a past one. Rotate both.
2
Callback Friday Topic
A module from 2–3 weeks ago that gets a 10-minute revisit. This is how you fight the forgetting curve without reteaching full lessons.
3
Check-Off / Milestone
Any skills check-off, cumulative review, or phase assessment that happens this week. Marks progress and catches gaps early.

Quick Reminders — The Four Retention Rhythms
⚡ Every Class · 3–5 min
Daily Recall Question — No notes, out loud. One concept from the current module OR a past one. This is the single highest-impact habit in this whole system. Don't skip it.
📅 Every Friday · 10 min
Callback Friday — Pick a topic from 2–3 weeks ago. One question, one scenario, one mnemonic review. Label it on your lesson plan so it never gets cut when time is tight.
✅ End of Each Unit
Unit Check-Off — Before moving to the next module, do a quick 5-item skills demonstration. Students who can't demonstrate Week 6 content in Week 8 will not be ready for the exam in Week 18.
🔄 Week 9 + Week 17
Cumulative Reviews — Two dedicated review sessions across the course. Not new content — just retrieval. Week 9 is your early warning system. Week 17 is your final simulation.
