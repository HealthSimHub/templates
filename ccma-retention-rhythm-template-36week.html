Template — 36 Weeks
A week-by-week retention planning template for year-long NHA CCMA courses — built around a 36-week 4x9 structure with four cumulative review points and a clinical rotation window.
NHA CCMA Aligned 36-Week / Full Year Clinical Rotation Aware 4 x 9-Week Quarters Print or Fill Digitally Free Resource
Teaching a year-long course? This is not the 18-week template stretched out. Year-long CCMA courses have a different retention challenge — content taught in September needs to still be accessible in April. This template builds in four quarterly review points, a deeper callback rotation, and a clinical rotation window. The module sequence is the same. The rhythm is different.
How to use this template: Fill in your current module for each week and your daily recall topic. The Callback Friday column already has guidance for how far back to reach — in a year-long course, you need to reach further than 2–3 weeks once you hit Q2 and beyond. The quarterly review weeks (W9, W18, W27, W35) are pre-marked — treat those as non-negotiable. Clinical rotation weeks are noted as a window — adjust based on your district's schedule.
The Four Columns
1
Daily Recall Topic
One concept, out loud, no notes, first 3–5 minutes of class. In a year-long course rotate between current AND older content daily — by Q3 students need to be recalling Q1 content regularly.
2
Callback Friday Topic
Reach back 3–6 weeks in Q1–Q2, and 6–10 weeks in Q3–Q4. The forgetting curve is steeper over a full year. If you only reach back 2–3 weeks in April, September content is gone.
3
Check-Off / Milestone
Four quarterly cumulative reviews are pre-placed. Each one covers everything taught so far — not just the current quarter. These are your insurance policy against the forgetting curve.
Year-Long Teaching — What's Different
📅 Quarterly Reviews — Non-Negotiable
In an 18-week course you can get away with one mid-point review. In a 36-week course, one review is not enough. Content from Q1 will be cold by Q3 without deliberate callbacks. Protect the four quarterly review weeks — don't use them for new content.
↩ Callback Distance Increases Over Time
In Q1, reach back 2–3 weeks. By Q3 and Q4, reach back 6–10 weeks. If you're in Week 28 and your Callback Friday topic is from Week 25, that's not a callback — that's just review. True spaced repetition requires reaching much further back as the course progresses.
🏥 Clinical Rotation Window
Clinical weeks are marked as a window around Weeks 28–33. Adjust to match your district's schedule. During clinical rotations, students are seeing content live — use your classroom time for recall and debrief rather than new instruction. The rotation IS the retention tool.
⚠ The Q3 Slide
Most year-long CCMA courses see a retention dip in Q3 — roughly Weeks 19–27. Students are past the novelty of clinical skills and haven't hit exam urgency yet. This is where daily recall matters most. Don't ease up on it in Q3 thinking you have time.

Year-Long Retention — The Four Rules
⚡ Daily · 3–5 min · Never Skip
Daily recall is more important in a year-long course than a semester course — because there's more time for content to go cold. One question, out loud, no notes. Rotate between current and older content starting in Q2.
📅 Quarterly · Non-Negotiable
Four cumulative reviews, not one. Weeks 9, 18, 27, and 35. Each one covers everything taught so far. In a year-long course, skipping a quarterly review means Q1 content is effectively gone by exam time.
↩ Callbacks — Reach Further in Q3/Q4
Callback distance must increase as the year goes on. Q1: reach back 2–3 weeks. Q2: reach back 4–6 weeks. Q3/Q4: reach back 8–10 weeks. If you're only reaching back 2 weeks in April, September content has been forgotten for months.
🏥 Clinical Rotation — Use It
Clinical weeks are not a break from retention. Students are seeing content live — debrief everything. Ask "what did you see this week and where does it connect to what we learned?" The rotation is your best retention tool of the entire year.
